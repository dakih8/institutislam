Zakat is one of the five pillars of Islam and is an obligatory charity on wealth that meets a minimum threshold (nisab) and has been held for a full lunar year. It is typically calculated at 2.5% of eligible savings and is distributed to specific categories of recipients defined in the Quran, including the poor and needy.
